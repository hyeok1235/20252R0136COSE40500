# San Jose State University & Silicon Valley Startup Ecosystem

**Speaker:** 편재호 교수 (Prof. Jaeho Pyeon), San Jose State University

**Mentorship Roles:** Silicon Valley KIC, Bootup World, ThinkTomi

## Speaker Background

Professor at San Jose State University with active involvement in Silicon Valley's startup ecosystem as a mentor across multiple organizations.

## Mentorship Organizations

### Silicon Valley KIC (Korea Innovation Center)
- Government-funded initiative supporting Korean startups in the U.S. market
- Established in 2014 in San Francisco Bay Area
- Supports 80+ Korean startups annually
- Programs: Digital Healthcare, AI & SaaS focused
- Offers PMF (Product-Market Fit) program with 1:1 mentoring using V2MOM framework

### ThinkTomi
- Silicon Valley-based startup accelerator and education company
- Public Benefit Corporation based in California
- Offers 20, 40, and 80-hour entrepreneurship programs
- Focus: Innovative thinking, entrepreneurship, transformational business education
- T-Global community connects alumni, mentors, and entrepreneurs worldwide

## Agents4Science 2025

**The 1st Open Conference of AI Agents for Science**

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Date | October 22, 2025 (virtual) |
| Organizers | Stanford University, Together AI |
| Unique Requirement | AI must be sole first author |
| Acceptance Rate | ~16% (24/300+ submissions) |
| Award | $10,000 compute credit for Outstanding Papers |

### Key Features
- First conference where AI authorship is required, not just allowed
- AI agents write papers and serve as first-round reviewers
- Human experts evaluate top papers for final awards
- Explores whether AI can independently generate novel scientific insights

### Conference Findings
- AI promises to speed scientific analysis and writing
- However, AI agents still struggle with accuracy and judgment in practice
